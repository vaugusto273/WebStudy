FAQ

  1. What programs and degrees does the college offer?

    Our college offers a variety of undergraduate and graduate programs across multiple disciplines, including Arts, Sciences, Engineering, Business, and Humanities. We also provide professional certificates and continuing education courses.

  2. How can I apply to the college?

    To apply, visit our official website and complete the online application form. You’ll need to submit transcripts, a personal statement, letters of recommendation, and, if applicable, standardized test scores (SAT, ACT, GRE, etc.). International students must also provide proof of English proficiency (TOEFL, IELTS).

  3. What are the admission requirements?

    Admission requirements vary by program. Generally, applicants must have a high school diploma (or equivalent) with a competitive GPA, relevant coursework, and strong letters of recommendation. Specific programs may require additional prerequisites or an entrance exam.

  4. What is the application deadline?

    Application deadlines differ depending on the program and the type of admission (early decision, regular decision, rolling admission). Check our admissions page for specific dates.

  5. How much is tuition, and are there financial aid options?

    Tuition varies by program and residency status (in-state vs. out-of-state). We offer various financial aid options, including scholarships, grants, work-study programs, and student loans. For detailed information, please visit our Financial Aid Office website.
